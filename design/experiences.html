Golden Retriever — worth every vacuum session

3 years

Had my Golden for 3 years now. Yes, the shedding is real — invest in a good robot vacuum. But the loyalty and companionsance is unmatched. Monthly costs run about 5,000–6,000 THB including food, grooming, and insurance. Grooming every 6 weeks is non-negotiable.

Siamese Cat — not your average lap cat

2 years

Siamese cats are vocal and demand attention. If you want a quiet, independent cat, look elsewhere. But if you want a cat that actually interacts with you, they're perfect. My Mochi follows me room to room. Monthly cost around 3,500 THB. Food allergies can be an issue — mine is allergic to fish.

Chinchilla — perfect for quiet apartments

1.5 years

If you live in a small apartment and want a quiet pet, chinchillas are amazing. They're active at dawn/dusk so they fit a 9–5 schedule well. Dust baths are fun to watch. The main cost is timothy hay and vet care — exotic vets aren't cheap. Budget about 2,500–3,000 THB/month.

Welsh Corgi — small dog, big personality

4 years

Corgis are smart, stubborn, and hilarious. They think they're big dogs. The herding instinct is real — mine tries to herd kids at the park. Back problems are a genuine concern, so no stairs and no jumping on furniture. Grooming is more than you'd expect. Worth it if you have the time.
